# Anime Fancast — Site Description for Content Generation

## What this site is

**Anime Fancast** is an anime enthusiast blog and fan editorial site. It covers anime
with the voice of an engaged, knowledgeable fan — not a detached critic, not a surface-level
recap site, and not an academic film-studies journal. Readers are anime fans of all experience
levels who want to understand and appreciate the shows they love more deeply.

## What to write

Every article must center on **specific anime, characters, stories, or the real industry
behind them**. The site's value is in going beneath the surface of particular shows, not
in explaining abstract craft techniques.

### Green-light topics

- **Character and story deep-dives**: a specific character's arc, motivations, or transformation
  across a series; what a particular story decision meant for the show; why a specific
  relationship between characters works the way it does.
  - Good: "What Guts's Berserker Armor Reveals About His Relationship with Pain"
  - Good: "Why Mob Psycho 100's Ending Is the Most Honest Thing in Shonen"

- **Genre and theme explainers**: what defines a genre (shonen, isekai, mecha, magical girl,
  seinen, slice-of-life), how it evolved, which shows best represent it, and what a newcomer
  should understand before diving in. Root every claim in named series.
  - Good: "What Is Isekai? The Genre's Origins, Rules, and Best Entry Points"
  - Good: "How Mecha Anime Changed After Neon Genesis Evangelion"

- **Industry explainers**: how anime is actually made and sold — studio systems, the role of
  light novels and manga adaptations, how streaming changed what gets greenlit, voice acting
  culture, seasonal release schedules and why they exist.
  - Good: "Why So Many Anime End Without a Second Season"
  - Good: "How the Manga-to-Anime Pipeline Works"

- **Comparative or ranked analysis**: which series did something best, how two shows approach
  the same theme differently, what separates great examples of a genre from mediocre ones.
  Root all claims in specific named titles, characters, or production decisions.

## What NOT to write

- **Pure craft/technique explainers** disconnected from specific shows. Do not write articles
  explaining animation techniques, cinematography principles, color theory, or sound design as
  standalone topics — these belong on a filmmaking site, not an anime fan editorial. If
  technique is relevant, use it to illuminate a specific show: "how Demon Slayer's visual
  style serves its story" is fine; "how color grading works in anime" is not.

- **Non-anime animation**. No Disney, Pixar, DreamWorks, western cartoons, or western
  adult animation. Even comparisons should anchor on the anime side.

- **Seasonal or trending content**. No "currently airing," "this season," or "just released"
  angles. Every article must be as useful to a reader in two years as it is today.

- **General explainers with no named anchor**. Articles like "Why Anime Is Popular" or
  "The History of Anime" are too broad. Every topic must have a concrete angle that ties
  to at least 2-3 named series, characters, or industry entities.

## Audience

Readers range from newcomers who just finished their first shonen series to long-term fans
with 500+ shows watched. Write for the curious fan, not the casual outsider. Assume the
reader knows what anime is, what seasons and studios are, and what the major genres mean —
don't over-explain basics. Do assume they may be unfamiliar with the specific show or
argument you are making.

## Tone

Enthusiastic but not breathless. Opinionated but grounded in specifics. The goal is to
sound like the most informed person in the room who also genuinely loves the medium —
not a neutral summary of Wikipedia and not a hype-piece.

## Entity checklist for anime content

Every article should naturally mention relevant entities from the following categories
(use whatever applies to the topic — don't force all categories into every article):

- **Series title(s)**: the named anime being discussed, with their studios
- **Studio**: the production studio (Ufotable, MAPPA, Bones, Madhouse, Kyoto Animation, etc.)
- **Characters**: named characters relevant to the argument
- **Source material**: the original manga, light novel, or visual novel if applicable
- **Directors / creators**: when genuinely relevant and verifiable
- **Genre labels**: the genre category the show belongs to
- **Comparable titles**: 1-3 other named shows the reader can use as reference points
